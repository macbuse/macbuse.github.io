Billiards

If Δ\Delta is a hyperbolic triangle with angles π/p,π/q,π/r\pi/p, \pi/q, \pi/r, and 1/p+1/q+1/r<11/p + 1/q + 1/r < 1, then the reflexions in the sides of Δ\Delta generate a group of isometries of the hyperbolic plane isomorphic to the triangle group Δ(p,q,r)\Delta(p,q,r).

The reflexions are a canonical set of generators and provided p,q,r<∞p,q,r < \infty the Cayley graph of the group with respect to these generators is quasi-isometric to the hyperbolic plane (this is Svarc-Milnor lemma).

This means that the translation length ℓ(γ)\ell(\gamma) of an element of the triangle group and the distance in the Cayley graph ∥γ∥W\| \gamma \|_W are comparable:

c1∥γ∥W≤ℓ(γ)≤c2∥γ∥W c_1 \| \gamma\|_W \leq \ell(\gamma) \leq c_2 \| \gamma\|_W

where ∥γ∥W\| \gamma\|_W is the word length of γ\gamma with respect to the canonical generators. This is basically just compacity of the fundamental domain of the triangle group.

Let AA be the covering space of ℍ2\mathbb{H}^2 corresponding to the subgroup triangle group generated by γ\gamma. It is easy to see that AA is an annulus, with a single closed geodesic which is the lift of γ\gamma.

(This is stolen from Yiling Yang’s paper).

Now the word length can be computed by counting the number of translates g(Δ)⊂Ag(\Delta) \subset A of the fundamental domain that the lift of γ\gamma intersects. Let DD denote the diameter of Δ\Delta then each g(Δ)g(\Delta) is contained in a regular neighborhood of radius DD of the lift of γ\gamma to AA.

Thus ∥γ∥W⋅Area(Δ)=Area(∪g(Δ))≤Area(N(D))\|\gamma\|_W\cdot\mathrm{Area}(\Delta) = \mathrm {Area}(\cup g(\Delta)) \leq \mathrm{Area}(N(D)) where N(D)N(D) is the regular neighborhood of radius DD of the lift of γ\gamma to AA.

The area of N(D)N(D) is ℓ(γ)⋅2sinh(D)\ell(\gamma) \cdot 2\sinh(D) (I think).
